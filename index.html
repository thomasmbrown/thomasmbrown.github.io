<div class="padd">

<p class="well-spaced">Since October 2018 I have been a PhD student at the University of Southampton under the supervision of

<a href ="https://www.southampton.ac.uk/maths/about/staff/ijl1y09.page" >Professor Ian Leary</a>.  Before I became a student at

Southampton, I completed an MMath degree at the University of South Wales.  In October 2021 I will be joining

<a href="https://people.maths.ox.ac.uk/kielak/">Dr Dawid Kielak's</a> team in the Mathematical Institute at the University of Oxford</p>

<p class="well-spaced">My research is primarily in geometric group theory, often with a topological flavour.  More specifically, I am interested in lattices in

locally compact groups, cohomology of arithmetic groups, \(L^2\)-invariants, and \(K\)-theory in relation to the isomorphism conjectures. </p>

<p class="well-spaced">My recent activities

can be found <a href="./activities.html">here</a>.</p>

<p class="well-spaced">I was one of the organisers of the interdisciplinary seminar series <a href="https://www.southampton.ac.uk/maths/research/groups/maths-and-mingle.page">Maths and Mingle</a>. 

You can read more about Maths and Mingle in our <a href="Misc/20_May_LMS_Newsletter.pdf">article</a> in the

May 2020 edition of the <a href="https://www.lms.ac.uk/publications/lms-newsletter-back-issues">LMS newsletter</a>.

I am also co-organising the conference <a href="https://sites.google.com/view/pgtc2020">Postgraduate Group Theory 2020 (PGTC 2020)</a>.</p>

<div class="padd">

                <h2>Preprints and publications</h2>

                <i> Click titles for abstracts.</i>

 

<ol reversed>

<li> <div onclick="displayabs('a6')">Hierarchically hyperbolic groups, products of \(\textrm{CAT(-1)}\) spaces, and

virtual torsion-freeness, <i>submitted</i>.</div>

<a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2105.02847">arXiv</a>, <a href="Preprints/2021bSH.pdf">pdf</a>.

<div class="padl"><div class="bord", id="a6">

We prove that a group acting geometrically on a product of proper minimal \(\textrm{CAT(-1)}\) spaces without

permuting isometric factors is a hierarchically hyperbolic group. As an application we construct hierarchically

hyperbolic groups which are not virtually torsion-free.

</div></div>

<br></li>

 

<li> <div onclick="displayabs('a5')">Graphs and complexes of lattices, <i>submitted</i>.</div>

<a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.13728">arXiv</a>, <a href="Preprints/2021aSH.pdf">pdf</a>, <a href="Misc/21_BMC_BAMC_Poster.pdf">poster</a>, <a href="Misc/040321_ETH_Zurich_JGS_Slides.pdf">slides</a>.

<div class="padl"><div class="bord", id="a5">

We study lattices acting on \(\textrm{CAT}(0)\) spaces via their commensurated

subgroups.  To do this we introduce the notions of a graph of lattices and a complex of

lattices giving graph and complex of group splittings of \(\textrm{CAT}(0)\) lattices. 

Using this framework we characterise irreducible uniform \((\textrm{Isom}(\mathbb{E}^n)\times T)\)-lattices

by \(C^\ast\)-simplicity and the failure of virtual fibring and biautomaticity.  We

construct non-residually finite uniform lattices acting on arbitrary products of

right angled buildings and non-biautomatic lattices acting on the product of

\(\mathbb{E}^n\) and a right-angled building.  We investigate the residual finiteness,

\(L^2\)-cohomology, and \(C^\ast\)-simplicity of \(\textrm{CAT}(0)\) lattices more

generally.  Along the way we prove that many right angled Artin groups with rank \(2\)

centre are not quasi-isometrically rigid.

</div></div>

<br></li>

 

<li> <div onclick="displayabs('a4')">(With <a href ="https://math.unice.fr/~indira/">Indira L. Chatterji</a>

and <a href="https://www.southampton.ac.uk/maths/about/staff/phk1e12.page">Peter H. Kropholler</a>) Groups

acting on trees and the first \(\ell^2\)-Betti number, <i>submitted</i>.</div>

<a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2012.13368">arXiv</a>,  <a href="Preprints/2020ICSHPK.pdf">pdf</a>.

<div class="padl"><div class="bord", id="a4">

                We generalise results of Thomas, Allcock, Thom-Petersen, and Kar-Niblo to the first \(\ell^2\)-Betti number of quotients of certain

                groups acting on trees by subgroups with free actions on the edge sets of the graphs.

</div></div>

<br></li>

 

<li> <div onclick="displayabs('a3')">On the equivariant \(K\)- and \(KO\)-homology of some special linear groups, to appear in <i>Algebraic and Geometric Topology</i>.</div>

<a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2004.08199">arXiv</a>,  <a href="Preprints/2020SH.pdf">pdf</a>.

<div class="padl"><div class="bord", id="a3">

                We compute the equivariant \(KO\)-homology of the classifying space for proper

actions of \(\textrm{SL}_3(\mathbb{Z})\) and \(\textrm{GL}_3(\mathbb{Z})\). We also

compute the Bredon homology and equivariant \(K\)-homology of the classifying

spaces for proper actions of \(\textrm{PSL}_2(\mathbb{Z}[\frac{1}{p}])\) and

\(\textrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}[\frac{1}{p}])\) for each prime \(p\). Finally, we prove

the Unstable Gromov-Lawson-Rosenberg Conjecture for a large class of groups

whose maximal finite subgroups are odd order and have periodic cohomology.

</div></div>

<br></li>

 

<li> <div onclick="displayabs('a2')">Cohomology of Fuchsian groups and non-Euclidean crystallographic groups, <i>submitted</i>.</div>

<a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1910.00519">arXiv</a>,  <a href="Preprints/2019SH.pdf">pdf</a>.

<div class="padl"><div class="bord", id="a2">

                                For each geometrically finite non-Euclidean crystallographic group (NEC group), we compute the cohomology groups.

                                In the case where the group is a Fuchsian group, we also determine the ring structure of the cohomology. Finally, we compute the \(L^2\)-Betti numbers of the NEC groups.

</div></div>

<br></li>

 

<li> <div onclick="displayabs('a1')">(With <a href="https://nickpgill.github.io/">Nick Gill</a>) The character table of a sharply \(5\)-transitive subgroup of the alternating group of degree \(12\), <i>International Journal of Group Theory</i> (2021) <b>10</b>, no.1, 11-30.</div>

<a href="https://dx.doi.org/10.22108/ijgt.2019.115366.1531">Journal</a>,  <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1806.09510">arXiv</a>,  <a href="Preprints/2018NGSH.pdf">pdf</a>.

<div class="padl"><div class="bord", id="a1">

                In this paper we calculate the character table of a sharply \(5\)-transitive subgroup of \({\rm Alt}(12)\), and of a sharply \(4\)-transitive subgroup of \( \rm{Alt}(11\)).

                Our presentation of these calculations is new because we make no reference to the sporadic simple Mathieu groups, and instead deduce the desired character tables using only the existence of the stated multiply transitive permutation representations.

</div></div>

<br></li>

</ol>
